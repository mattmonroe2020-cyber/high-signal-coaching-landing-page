Honest about who this works for

Most coaching is built for people who already have consistent follow-through. This isn't that.

This is for you if —

  • You're a startup founder or senior tech lead with ADHD, autism, or both
  • You have high cognitive horsepower but inconsistent follow-through
  • You've read every productivity book and nothing sticks longer than two weeks
  • You lose hours to task paralysis, then hyperfocus until 2am on the wrong thing
  • Rejection sensitivity (RSD) derails your team dynamics and investor relationships

This is not for you if —

FAQ

Questions worth asking

Is this therapy?
No. High Signal Coaching is coaching — not psychotherapy. While I am a licensed therapist, this engagement is a coaching relationship governed by a coaching agreement, not a clinical or therapeutic relationship. We focus on executive function, follow-through, and business performance — not on processing trauma or treating mental health conditions. If you're in acute distress or need clinical support, I'll refer you to the right resource.
Do I need an ADHD or autism diagnosis?
No formal diagnosis is required. Many clients are self-identified, late-diagnosed, or "AuDHD" (both). What matters is whether your experience matches the profile: high cognitive capacity, inconsistent follow-through, executive dysfunction patterns, and a brain that doesn't respond to standard productivity advice. The fit call is where we assess that together.
What does "IFS-informed" mean in a coaching context?
IFS (Internal Family Systems) is a well-researched therapeutic model developed by Richard Schwartz. In coaching, I use the parts framework — not as therapy — but as a lens for understanding why intelligent, motivated founders repeatedly hit the same internal walls. We identify the protective parts (the procrastinator, the perfectionist, the taskmaster) and work to change their relationship to your actual goals. It's precise, not woo.
Do you use AI tools? How is my data handled?
Yes — I use AI tools for things like research synthesis, worksheet generation, and building your external-brain systems. These tools are used to serve your work, not to analyze or store your personal disclosures. Session content is kept confidential. No client-identifying information is processed through AI tools without your explicit knowledge. If you have specific data-handling requirements (e.g., you're in a regulated industry), we'll discuss that in our first session.
How long does the program run?
The core engagement is 8 modules, typically completed over 90 days. Pacing is adjusted to your workload — I'm not interested in adding another thing to manage. After the core program, some clients continue with ongoing support; others have what they need. We decide that together at the end of Module 8.
What's the time commitment per week?
One structured session per week (60 minutes). Between sessions: short daily check-ins (15 minutes or less), using systems we build together in the early modules. The program is designed to reduce your cognitive load, not increase it. We account for ADHD-adjusted time reality — neurotypical estimates multiplied by 2.5.
What's the investment?
Pricing is discussed on the fit call, where we assess scope, timing, and what level of support makes sense. I don't publish rates publicly because context matters — a solo founder in pre-seed is in a different position than a Series A team lead. The fit call is free, no-pitch, and takes 30 minutes.
